# Pentaport 2019 cause dossier

Created: 2026-07-08T02:08:00+09:00

Case: 2019 Incheon Pentaport Rock Festival / Incheon Pentaport Music Festival.

Why this case matters:
- It is the first dossier where a public event-outcome critique can be connected to the festival's operator-selection and joint-operator governance structure.
- It is not enough to say "agency caused failure"; the evidence supports "operator transition and governance/selection process were plausible risk factors."

Evidence chain:
- Incheon Institute report: Incheon City delegates to ITO; ITO selects a private joint operator; the private operator handles production, artist management, marketing, venue/site, operations, safety, administration, revenue and settlement.
- Public reporting: the operator changed after Yescom's contract period, Gyeonggi Ilbo was selected through public bidding, a participant filed an injunction to stop the bidding procedure, and the court dismissed the injunction.
- Public reporting: ITO proceeded with the implementation agreement despite the injunction.
- Public critique: lineup freshness, announcement/communication, timetable, value perception, and ticket-sale concerns were reported by music media/public commentary.

Current conclusion:
- Classification: Level A causal candidate, not proven final cause.
- Strongest hypothesis: governance/operator-transition risk plus programming/communication strategy weakness.
- Open evidence gap: official 2019 evaluation report, ITO scoring documents, agreement, settlement, visitor count, satisfaction survey, complaint logs.
