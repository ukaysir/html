# Yeongdeok Snow Crab Festival price/brand-control dossier

Observed at: 2026-07-08

## Verdict

Yeongdeok Snow Crab Festival is a price-governance and brand-protection prevention case. The important causal mechanism is not a proven contractor failure; it is a public-market coordination response to a commodity-price risk.

## Why this matters

- Snow crab is a live commodity, so price fluctuates with supply and demand. This makes fixed pricing difficult and creates high overcharge perception risk.
- Yeongdeok's response traces a prevention chain: market-price display, pre-attached booth price labels, merchant/Suhyup/foundation/county agreement, complaint systems, citizen monitoring, expanded tasting/experience crab supply, smart queueing and local voucher/refund leads.
- This is useful as a comparator for festivals where price controversy broke out: it shows what controls need to exist before a festival can credibly claim price/booth governance.

## Causal guardrail

Do not credit or blame a hidden agency/subcontractor. Current public evidence shows organizer, foundation, festival committee, merchants and Suhyup coordination. No public source found in this pass connects a specific outsourced agency to the price-control outcome.
