# Pentaport 2025 success dossier

Created: 2026-07-08T02:19:00+09:00

Case: 2025 Incheon Pentaport Rock Festival / 20th anniversary.

Why this case matters:
- It is the first success-side dossier that pairs demand outcome, satisfaction, crowd/heat countermeasures, content programming, and local-commerce measures.
- It is a useful contrast to the 2019 Pentaport dossier: the same festival family shows how governance/operation evidence must be tied to outcome evidence before drawing conclusions.

Evidence chain:
- Official Incheon page reports 166,000 visitors based on KT mobile big-data analysis, about KRW 83.6 billion direct economic effect, and satisfaction 5.83/7.
- Official/public reports name safety consulting, expanded large tent cooling zones, Pulp's first Korea performance, donation/local F&B, and 20th anniversary exhibition as operating factors.
- Public reporting says the 2025 festival ranked first among 31 summer festivals in a consumer satisfaction survey by Financial News and Korea Research.
- EToday report adds operational details: all festival dates used overseas headliners, safety consulting, cooling zone expansion to 2,225 square meters, 35,000 free water bottles, emergency medical stations, and emergency evacuation routes.

Current conclusion:
- Classification: Level C success outcome with strong official outcome evidence.
- Do not assign success to a contractor yet. The sources identify Incheon City, ITO, and Gyeonggi Ilbo as host/co-organizers, but contract-level responsibility and task split still need official procurement/settlement records.
