# Cheongsong Apple Festival metric-opacity success dossier

Observed at: 2026-07-08

## Verdict

Cheongsong Apple Festival is a success/control case, not a failure case. The causal value is that success metrics themselves need audit separation: offline visitors, online visits/search exposure, direct product sales, direct economic effect, indirect effect and satisfaction are different claims.

## Why this matters for the national festival model

- It shows how a rural product festival can combine participatory content, hybrid online access, price-control measures and local product sales.
- It also shows why headline economic-effect numbers should not be ingested as a single truth: public reporting used multiple figures and a local report questioned visitor-count omission and formula transparency.
- The 2025/2026 stage and performance agency procurement is a real outsourcing context, but current evidence does not connect a contractor to either success or metric opacity.

## Key source observations

- KTO lists 2026 dates, free entry with paid partial experiences, operator as Cheongsong County / Cheongsong Festival Promotion Committee, and menu prices.
- Cheongsong County's tourism page lists the 2025 date, host/supervisor split, free admission, hybrid online structure and participatory programs.
- Cheongsong pledge PDF states total project cost KRW 4.49b for 2022-2026 and identifies local resident participation constraints and aging-related foreign-reception limits as problems.
- Bid notices for 2025 and 2026 stage/performance program agency services show KRW 410m packages and state that the county only handles bid notice agency while contract, supervision and payment belong to the Cheongsong Festival Promotion Committee.
- Yonhap / county evaluation reporting says 2025 direct economic effect KRW 47.1b-class, indirect KRW 97.2b-class, satisfaction 6.06/7, and price-fixed sales/complaint-center/self-evaluation systems were positively assessed.
- Daegu/Daum reporting gives granular survey figures: 86.4% outside visitors, 64.6% repeat visitors, 53.8% lodging among outside visitors, 1-person average spending KRW 152k, direct effect KRW 47.17b-class.
- LocalSegye raised a counter-signal about earlier KRW 30b / KRW 60b effect reporting: official visitor count around 500k was omitted from that release, formulas were not disclosed, and online visitor numbers may be mistaken for real visits.

## Guardrail

Do not label this as waste or contractor failure. Label it as success with metric-opacity and procurement-context risk until official evaluation report formulas, Local Finance 365 rows and contract performance evaluations are joined.
