# Festival governance causal chain wave 1

Created: 2026-07-08T01:58:00+09:00

Purpose: separate "contract/procurement evidence exists" from "contract/procurement governance can plausibly explain success/failure."

Rule:
- Level A: outcome/incident + stakeholder/procurement structure + public critique or official record all connect.
- Level B: official procurement chain exists, but outcome issue is not yet attached.
- Level C: event success/outcome exists, but procurement responsibility is not yet attached.
- Level D: only event page or only mirror evidence exists.

Findings:
- Incheon Pentaport 2019 is the strongest Level A case in this wave: public reporting connects joint-operator selection dispute, injunction attempt, agreement execution, lineup/communication dissatisfaction, and broader planning criticism.
- Incheon Pentaport 2025 is a Level C success case: visitor count, satisfaction score, safety/heat-response and content/local-commerce measures are publicly reported, but contractor-specific responsibility still needs official linkage.
- Bucheon 2025 is Level B: official tender and evaluation-result chain exists, but a success/failure outcome claim has not yet been attached.
- Suwon/Cheorwon are procurement-structure leads; do not yet claim contractor-caused success/failure.
