# Gwangyang Maehwa Festival traffic/demand redesign dossier

Observed at: 2026-07-08

## Verdict

Gwangyang Maehwa Festival is a demand/crowd/traffic redesign case. It is not a proven contractor failure. The public evidence shows a persistent geographic capacity problem that the city tried to manage with bloom forecasting, car-free access, expanded parking, shuttle buses, real-time traffic information, paid-entry/local-voucher conversion and citywide dispersal.

## Why this matters

- The festival has historically attracted very large spring-flower demand, while the Maehwa Village site has constrained roads and parking.
- The problem is therefore not simply "too many tourists"; it is a capacity matching problem: timing forecast, road entry control, parking inventory, last-mile shuttles, accessibility vehicles, information flow, food/price controls and local-spend loops.
- The 2026 result report is a useful success/control lead: visitors nearly doubled year over year, satisfaction rose, and the city attributed performance to accumulated-temperature bloom prediction, timing adjustment, content and traffic management.

## Guardrail

Do not credit or blame a contractor. Current public sources identify public planning and festival committee/municipal operation signals, not a vendor-to-outcome chain.
