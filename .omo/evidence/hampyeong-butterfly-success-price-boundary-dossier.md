# Hampyeong Butterfly success / price-boundary dossier evidence note

Scope: public-source-only dossier for Hampyeong Butterfly Festival, 2023-2026.

Working verdict: success benchmark with a price-boundary risk. The festival has paid ecological/participatory content, admission revenue, coupon/local-spend loop, and local sales evidence. The 2023 fishcake-price controversy is better classified as nearby/private night-market boundary-control failure, not total festival failure and not proven subcontractor failure.

Core evidence:
- KTO Visit Korea official festival page: 2026 dates, paid entry, operator Hampyeong County / Hampyeong Festival Tourism Foundation, performance and ecological-experience program mix, public food-price listing.
- Yonhap 2026 wrap-up: 236,000 visitors, KRW 830m admission revenue, KRW 286m local agricultural-specialty sales, KRW 900m booth/convenience-facility sales, participatory ecological programming.
- The Fact/Daum 2026 advance-ticket report: adult/youth coupon return usable at booths and local shops, 10% pre-sale discount, linked ecological facilities discount.
- Yeongnam/Daum 2026 fiscal comparison lead: 2024 execution KRW 1.3859b, 116,495 paid visitors, KRW 585.5m admission revenue, 42.2% recovery rate, local-currency loop. Treat as B lead until Local Finance 365 row is joined.
- Yonhap 2023 price controversy: fishcake bowl KRW 10,000 near festival; county said it was private land/night-market outside Expo Park but admitted price enforcement gap and promised broader hygiene/price checks.
- KCI 2013 success-factor study: persuasive theme, resident participation, structured organization and theme-fit infrastructure; operations expertise caveat.
- Hampyeong Foundation/Sankun tender leads: 2026 stage installation/operation tender exists, with third-party bid summary indicating KRW 90m budget and KRW 87m award to Open Planning. This is procurement context only; it does not prove responsibility for price controversy or success.

Causal guardrail: contractor/subcontractor causality requires outcome evidence + contract/RFP scope + actor identity + time/place match. Current evidence satisfies event/operator/procurement context but not blame attribution.
